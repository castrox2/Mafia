-----------------------------------------------     What Do They Do❓     -----------------------------------------------



-----------------------------------------------     Townsfolke❗     -----------------------------------------------

        Concept: 
            - The core Good-aligned team. They have positive, information-gathering, or protective abilities to help find the Demon.

        Key Rules: 
            - They do not know each other. They wake up individually at night to use their ability.
            - Their abilities are (mostly) true and helpful, but can be interfered with by poisoning/drunkenness or demon abilities.

            - Goal: Execute the Demon to win.

-----------------------------------------------     Outsiders❗     -----------------------------------------------
        Concept: 
            - Good-aligned characters whose abilities are harmful or misleading to the Good team. 
              Their presence is a drawback caused by certain Townsfolk (e.g., the Saint adds an extra Outsider).

        Key Rules:
            - They are on the Good team and win with Good.
            - They often don't know they are an Outsider (e.g., the Drunk believes they are a Townsfolk).
            - Their existence is a key piece of "world info" for the Good team to decipher.

-----------------------------------------------     Minions❗     ----------------------------------------------- 
        Concept: 
            - The Evil-aligned support team. They know the Demon and each other and have abilities to mislead, disrupt, or protect the Demon.

        Key Rules:
            - They know who the Demon and fellow Minions are (the "Evil team").
            - They wake together at the start of the game for this recognition.
            - They do not win if they are alive at the end. They win only if the Demon wins.

            - Goal: Protect the Demon and sow confusion.

-----------------------------------------------     Demons❗     -----------------------------------------------
        Concept: 
            - The primary Evil player. They kill one player each night and usually have a powerful misinformation ability.

        Key Rules:
            - They know who the Minions are (but Minions do not necessarily know which player is the Demon, unless the Demon is the one who kills).
            - They are the only evil player who kills at night.
            - If the Demon is executed, Good wins immediately.

            - Goal: Survive to achieve numerical parity with Good players ("evil majority") or eliminate all Townsfolk/Outsiders.

-----------------------------------------------     Traveler❗     -----------------------------------------------
        Concept: 
            - Variable-aligned players who can join or leave an in-progress game without breaking it.

        Key Rules:
            - They are not in the Grimoire. They are given a character sheet upon joining.
            - Their character and alignment (Good or Evil) is publicly announced.
            - The living players vote on whether to allow a new Traveler to join.
            - They do not die; they "depart." Upon departure, they use a powerful one-time ability.
            - They visit a player each night, making them interactable by night abilities.

-----------------------------------------------     Fabled❗     -----------------------------------------------
        Concept: 
            - Special characters that are never placed in the Grimoire. They add a unique, global rule that affects the entire game.

        Key Rules:
            - They are not assigned to any player. Their sheet is placed in the center of play.
            - They are controlled solely by the Storyteller.
            - They modify core game rules (e.g., The Storyteller may break the rules, The Angel protects a new player on their first day).
            - Used to balance custom scripts, fix edge cases, or introduce a novel twist.

-----------------------------------------------     Loric❗     -----------------------------------------------
    (May be wrong or off due to the fact that they are a weird "role")
        Concept: 
            - Advanced Storyteller characters that introduce a single, powerful, and disruptive global rule to fundamentally warp the game. 
            - Their purpose is not to solve problems but to create exciting new ones, transforming familiar scripts into fresh, challenging experiences.

        Key Rules:
            - They are never assigned to any player. Their character sheet is placed publicly as a permanent game condition.
            - They are controlled solely by the Storyteller and cannot be removed by any player action, vote, or ability.
            - Their effect is active for the entire game, altering core assumptions and strategies from the start.
            - They are designed to be permanently integrated into a specific script (e.g., "Trouble Brewing with The Hellmouth") to create a deliberate, high-concept variant.

            - Philosophy: "Twist over Fix." While Fabled characters smooth the game, Loric characters reshape it.

        Example:

            - Rule: "The Demon is an Outsider. If a player becomes the Demon, they are now an Outsider, and a Minion becomes the Demon."

            - Effect: This introduces a massive new problem. The core assumption that the Demon is on the Evil team is shattered. 
                      The Good team must now deduce which Outsider is secretly the killer, and the Evil team has a bizarre, public-facing Demon. 
                      It warps every piece of logic in the game.
